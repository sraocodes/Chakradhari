What We Do

We develop computational evidence pipelines that quantify whether soil and watershed interventions produce measurable change.

Government programs and research institutes implement watershed restoration and soil health interventions, but outcomes are rarely quantified rigorously. Soil properties change slowly. Short-term measurements can be misleading. Attribution requires multi-year baselines and careful statistical analysis.

Our work focuses on developing computational workflows that integrate field observations with satellite remote sensing and process-based models. We apply time-series analysis, uncertainty quantification, and model calibration techniques to produce defensible assessments of intervention effectiveness.

We specialize in multi-year engagements where computational rigor and data validation are required. We do not take short-term visualization projects or produce dashboards without validated underlying models.

Our Services

Three types of computational consulting engagements

01

Quantitative Evaluation & Model-Based Evidence

The Question: Did this soil or watershed intervention produce measurable outcomes?

Our Approach: We develop site-specific computational models calibrated against multi-year field data. We apply statistical hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification to assess intervention impacts. Results include effect size estimates with confidence bounds and identification of confounding variables.

Typical Clients: Research institutes evaluating watershed restoration programs. Government departments assessing multi-year soil health initiatives. Development agencies measuring climate adaptation outcomes.

This represents the majority of our consulting work.

02

Integrated Computational Workflows & Data Science Pipelines

The Problem: Your organization collects field data but lacks the computational infrastructure to convert observations into research-grade evidence.

What We Deliver: We build automated data processing pipelines that integrate field measurements with satellite observations. This includes quality control algorithms, gap-filling procedures, and model calibration workflows. Where data gaps exist, we apply validated estimation methods or design targeted measurement campaigns.

Typical Clients: Research groups needing automated boundary segmentation for farm-scale analysis. Institutes with incomplete time-series requiring statistical gap-filling methods. Monitoring programs seeking reproducible data processing protocols.

We handle the computational infrastructure so research teams can focus on scientific questions.

03

Decision Support Reports & Policy-Relevant Modeling

The Question: Does our climate or agricultural policy have empirical support for its claimed effectiveness?

What We Deliver: We conduct retrospective analysis of policy interventions using available monitoring data and satellite observations. Results are presented as technical reports with uncertainty estimates and methodological documentation suitable for policy review processes.

Typical Clients: Government agencies evaluating long-term climate adaptation programs. Policy teams requiring independent technical assessment of intervention outcomes.

These engagements build on established research relationships and require institutional commitment to evidence-based evaluation.

Project Scope: We focus on engagements of at least 6 months where computational modeling and data validation are central to the work. We do not produce standalone visualization tools or conduct projects where the primary deliverable is a dashboard without underlying model validation.

Technical Expertise

Specialized capabilities in environmental sensing and computational hydrology

Long-Term Field Monitoring & Data Analysis

Our team has managed high-frequency climate and soil monitoring networks across multiple agricultural catchments, including the Berambadi watershed in India. We understand the practical challenges of long-term environmental data: sensor calibration drift, seasonal variability, and incomplete records. This experience informs our approach to quality control and uncertainty estimation in all modeling work.

Computational Modeling of Root-Soil-Water Interactions

We apply non-invasive sensing techniques combined with inverse modeling to quantify subsurface water movement and root water uptake. These methods provide higher spatial resolution than traditional point measurements, enabling more accurate assessment of irrigation efficiency and crop water stress. The computational approaches we use are validated against field observations under varying soil conditions.

Satellite Data Processing & Calibration

We develop retrieval algorithms that convert multi-spectral and synthetic aperture radar observations into quantitative soil moisture and vegetation indices. Our methods include site-specific calibration using ground measurements to improve accuracy beyond generic satellite products. This allows regional-scale monitoring while maintaining field-validated precision where ground truth data permits.

Integrated Hydrologic Models & Scenario Analysis

We build decision support systems that couple physically-based hydrologic models with machine learning techniques for parameter estimation. These systems enable scenario analysis: testing how soil management or climate shifts might affect watershed-scale water balance over multi-year timescales. Model predictions include uncertainty bounds derived from ensemble methods and sensitivity analysis.

Our technical approach emphasizes validation against field observations. We focus on producing results that are reproducible and defensible under scientific review.

Methodological Approach

From field observations to validated computational models

Field Instrumentation & Ground Truth Data

We design and deploy environmental sensor networks for soil moisture, temperature, and hydrologic flux measurements. High temporal resolution observations serve as calibration targets for computational models and as validation datasets for satellite retrievals. Sensor placement follows sampling theory to capture spatial variability while maintaining operational feasibility.

Soil Moisture Networks Hydrologic Monitoring Quality Control Protocols

Remote Sensing Analysis & Algorithm Development

We process optical and radar satellite data to derive quantitative soil and vegetation parameters. Our retrieval algorithms are calibrated using ground observations and validated through independent field campaigns. This approach bridges the gap between satellite footprint scales and field measurement resolutions, enabling cost-effective regional monitoring where ground networks are infeasible.

SAR Processing Multi-Spectral Analysis Algorithm Validation

Process-Based Hydrologic Modeling

We apply physics-based watershed models calibrated using field and satellite observations. These models allow scenario testing: evaluating how land management changes or climate shifts might affect soil moisture, runoff, and crop water availability over multi-year periods. Results include uncertainty estimates derived from parameter sensitivity analysis and ensemble modeling approaches.

Why Chakradhari

Evaluation of soil and watershed interventions is often limited by either spatial scale or temporal resolution. Field studies provide detailed measurements but cover limited areas. Satellite observations offer regional coverage but require ground validation. Effective evaluation requires integrating both approaches within a computational framework that accounts for measurement uncertainty.

Our work combines environmental physics, statistical inference, and high-performance computing. This allows us to produce assessments that meet scientific review standards while addressing practical questions relevant to resource management and policy.

Technical Background

Our team has experience in inverse problems, data assimilation, and environmental physics. This ensures that computational models respect physical constraints and uncertainty estimates reflect actual measurement limitations, not just statistical convenience.

Field-Validated Methods

We specialize in data-limited environments where noise levels are high and measurement campaigns must be carefully designed. Our computational methods are developed for real-world agricultural landscapes, not idealized research settings.

Collaborative Approach

By limiting concurrent engagements, we provide substantial technical integration with client teams. We function as computational partners embedded within research or policy groups rather than as external contractors producing standalone deliverables.

Long-Term Direction

Our goal is to develop reusable computational infrastructure for environmental evaluation that can be applied across multiple geographic regions. Current projects involve site-specific model development. As our validated dataset library grows, we expect to reduce the time required to deploy calibrated models in new study areas.

The long-term objective is to establish a reference database for soil and water resource assessments. This would allow more rapid evaluation of new interventions by leveraging existing validation work, creating computational efficiency that scales with project portfolio size.

Open Methods & Reproducibility

We believe computational methods used for policy-relevant evaluation should be transparent and reproducible. Where possible, we release open-source processing modules for satellite data analysis and field data quality control. This approach helps establish methodological standards across the research community while ensuring our proprietary modeling systems remain compatible with evolving best practices.
