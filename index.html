What We Do

We design computational systems that process real-world data, run physics-aware simulations, and generate evidence for high-stakes decisions.

Research institutes and government programs implement interventions but rarely quantify outcomes rigorously. Physical systems change slowly. Short-term measurements mislead. Attribution requires multi-year data and careful statistical work. Most organizations lack the computational infrastructure to do this properly.

Our work integrates field observations with satellite data and process-based models. We apply time-series analysis, uncertainty quantification, and model calibration to produce defensible assessments. Our current projects focus on soil and climate systems—where validation is brutal and data collection is essential—but the same methods apply to industrial sensing, healthcare monitoring, and other data-intensive research domains.

We take multi-year engagements where computational rigor matters. We don't do short-term visualization projects or dashboards without validated models underneath.

Our Services

Three types of research consulting engagements

01

Model-Data Coupling & Evidence Generation

The Question: Did this intervention produce measurable outcomes, or are we seeing noise and confounding variables?

Our Approach: We build site-specific computational models calibrated against multi-year field data. We apply statistical hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification to assess impact. Results include effect size estimates with confidence bounds and identification of confounding factors. Most current engagements focus on environmental and climate programs, but the approach applies to any physical system with sparse measurement data.

Typical Clients: Research institutes evaluating long-term programs. Government departments assessing policy effectiveness. Development agencies measuring climate adaptation outcomes.

This represents the majority of our consulting work.

02

Integrated Data Processing & Simulation Pipelines

The Problem: Your organization collects field data but lacks the computational infrastructure to turn observations into research-grade evidence.

What We Deliver: Automated data processing pipelines that integrate field measurements with satellite observations and simulation models. This includes quality control algorithms, gap-filling methods, and model calibration workflows. Where data gaps exist, we apply validated estimation techniques or design targeted measurement campaigns.

Typical Clients: Research groups needing automated boundary detection for farm-scale analysis. Institutes with incomplete time-series requiring statistical gap-filling. Monitoring programs seeking reproducible processing protocols.

We build the computational infrastructure so research teams can focus on scientific questions.

03

Decision Support & Policy-Relevant Analysis

The Question: Does our policy have empirical support for its claimed effectiveness?

What We Deliver: Retrospective analysis of policy interventions using available monitoring data and satellite observations. Results are presented as technical reports with uncertainty estimates and methodological documentation suitable for policy review.

Typical Clients: Government agencies evaluating long-term programs. Policy teams requiring independent technical assessment of intervention outcomes.

These engagements build on established research relationships and require institutional commitment to evidence-based evaluation.

Project Scope: We focus on engagements of at least 6 months where computational modeling and data validation are central. We don't produce standalone visualization tools or dashboards without underlying model validation.

Technical Capabilities

Demonstrated through environmental and climate systems

Long-Term Field Monitoring & Data Validation

We design and operate high-frequency sensor networks for physical system monitoring. Our team has managed climate and soil monitoring networks across multiple catchments, including the Berambadi watershed in India. We understand sensor calibration drift, seasonal variability, and incomplete records. This experience informs our approach to quality control and uncertainty estimation across all projects.

Inverse Problems & Parameter Estimation

We apply non-invasive sensing techniques combined with inverse modeling to quantify subsurface processes. These methods provide higher spatial resolution than traditional point measurements. The computational approaches we use are validated against field observations under varying conditions and are applicable to any physical system where direct measurement is difficult or expensive.

Multi-Scale Data Fusion & Remote Sensing

We develop retrieval algorithms that convert satellite observations into quantitative physical parameters. Our methods include site-specific calibration using ground measurements to improve accuracy beyond generic products. This enables regional-scale monitoring while maintaining field-validated precision where ground truth data permits. The same data fusion principles apply to industrial sensing and medical imaging.

Physics-Aware Simulation & Scenario Analysis

We build decision support systems that couple process-based models with statistical learning for parameter estimation. These systems enable scenario testing: evaluating how management changes or external conditions might affect system behavior over multi-year timescales. Model predictions include uncertainty bounds derived from ensemble methods and sensitivity analysis.

Our approach emphasizes validation against real-world observations. We produce results that are reproducible and defensible under technical review.

Why Chakradhari

Evaluation of real-world interventions is often limited by either spatial scale or temporal resolution. Field studies provide detailed measurements but cover limited areas. Remote observations offer broad coverage but require ground validation. Effective evaluation requires integrating both within a computational framework that accounts for measurement uncertainty and data gaps.

Our work combines physical modeling, statistical inference, and computational methods. This allows us to produce assessments that meet technical review standards while addressing practical questions relevant to resource management and policy.

Technical Background

Our team has experience in inverse problems, data assimilation, and physical system modeling. This ensures computational models respect physical constraints and uncertainty estimates reflect actual measurement limitations, not statistical convenience.

Field-Validated Methods

We specialize in data-limited environments where noise levels are high and measurement campaigns must be carefully designed. Our computational methods are developed for real-world conditions, not idealized research settings.

Research Partnership Model

By limiting concurrent engagements, we provide deep technical integration with client teams. We function as computational partners embedded within research or policy groups rather than external contractors producing standalone deliverables.

Long-Term Direction

Our goal is to develop reusable computational infrastructure for physical system evaluation that can be deployed across multiple domains. Current projects involve site-specific model development in environmental systems. As our validated dataset library grows, we expect to reduce deployment time for calibrated models in new study areas and application domains.

The long-term objective is to establish a reference database for physical system assessments. This would allow rapid evaluation of new interventions by leveraging existing validation work, creating computational efficiency that scales with project portfolio size across environmental, industrial, and other application areas.

Open Methods & Reproducibility

We believe computational methods used for policy-relevant evaluation should be transparent and reproducible. Where possible, we release open-source processing modules for data analysis and quality control. This approach helps establish methodological standards across research communities while ensuring our proprietary systems remain compatible with evolving best practices.
