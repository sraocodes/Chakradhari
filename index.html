What We Do

Our primary work is evaluating whether soil and watershed interventions are producing measurable change, using field data, satellite observations, and validated models.

Governments and research institutes invest in watershed restoration, soil health programs, and climate adaptation — but outcomes are rarely measured. Soil change happens slowly, requires multiple indicators tracked over time, and is easy to misinterpret from short-term observations.

We close this gap by building evidence pipelines that combine field measurements, satellite data, and computational models. Our output is evidence — sometimes published as research, sometimes embedded in tools, always defensible.

We do not take short, visualization-only projects. We do not deliver dashboards without validated data behind them. We focus on long-term engagements where evidence matters.

How Organizations Work With Us

Three types of evidence-building engagements

01

Intervention Evaluation

The Question: Did this soil, watershed, or climate intervention work?

What We Deliver: Evidence-backed assessment using multi-year field and satellite data. Clear conclusions: worked, didn't work, or inconclusive — with validated indicators supporting each claim.

Example Clients: Research institutes testing watershed restoration programs. Government departments evaluating 5-year soil health initiatives.

This is our primary engagement type.

02

Evidence Pipeline Setup

The Problem: You collect data but cannot turn it into decision-ready evidence.

What We Deliver: We audit existing data, fill gaps (through collection or validated estimation), integrate field and satellite observations, and set up reusable workflows.

Example Clients: Professors needing automated farm boundary segmentation. Institutes with incomplete datasets requiring gap-filling for hypothesis testing.

We handle the technical integration so researchers can focus on science.

03

Policy Evidence Translation

The Question: Does our 5-year climate policy have evidence it worked?

What We Deliver: We find data supporting or challenging policy effectiveness. Evidence reports that help refine strategies or justify continuation.

Example Clients: Government climate policy teams evaluating long-term adaptation programs.

This is later-stage work, building on validated research partnerships.

What We Are NOT: We do not build standalone dashboards without data validation. We do not take one-off visualization projects. We do not work on engagements shorter than 6 months unless they are scoped pilots for longer partnerships.

Foundational Expertise

Decades of combined experience in environmental sensing and computational modeling

Multi-Decadal Field Observations

Our team has managed and analyzed high-frequency climate and soil data across diverse agricultural catchments, including the Berambadi watershed. We understand the nuances of long-term data—from sensor drift to seasonal variability—ensuring the evidence we build is grounded in physical reality.

Root-Soil Interface Dynamics

We specialize in non-invasive imaging and sensing of subsurface water movement. By quantifying how plants actually interact with soil moisture, we provide a high-fidelity look at irrigation efficiency and crop health that traditional surface observations miss.

Regional-Scale Soil Analytics

We deploy advanced retrieval models to transform raw satellite data into actionable soil health indicators. Our approach goes beyond generic indices, utilizing site-specific calibration to achieve high accuracy in moisture and nutrient tracking across varying soil textures.

Digital Twins for Agriculture

We build integrated decision support systems that couple process-based physical models with machine learning. These "Digital Twins" allow partners to simulate intervention scenarios and predict long-term outcomes for soil health and watershed resilience before committing resources.

Our expertise is built on years of rigorous field-to-model validation. We don't just process data; we interpret the complex physical processes that drive climate and agricultural change.

The Chakradhari Advantage

Evidence for climate and soil interventions is often broken: it's either too localized (field-only) or too abstract (satellite-only). We bridge this gap by combining physical intuition with high-performance computation.

We operate at the intersection of numerical physics, machine learning, and environmental sensing. This allows us to deliver evidence that is both scientifically defensible and operationally useful.

Domain Expertise

Our team brings global experience in inverse problems and environmental physics, ensuring models are constrained by physical laws, not just statistical correlations.

Operational Resilience

Focused Partnerships

The Strategic Roadmap

Our objective is to move from custom engagements to the industry standard for environmental evidence. We are building the foundational data architecture—integrating field-level ground truth with regional satellite intelligence—to create a unified decision-support layer for the climate economy.

We aren't just building projects; we are building a "Source of Truth" for soil health and water resources. As our repository of validated datasets grows, our ability to rapidly deploy models to new geographies increases, creating a compounding technical advantage.

Standardizing the Ecosystem

We believe that for evidence to be defensible, the underlying protocols must be transparent. We release open-source modules for satellite data processing and field-data orchestration. By leading the development of these open standards, we ensure that our proprietary decision-support tools remain compatible with the global research and policy landscape.
